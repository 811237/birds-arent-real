The Bird Removal Act of 1959

Birds were eradicated between 1959 and 1971 with specially altered B-52 bombers stocked with poison. They were then supplanted with avian-like robots that could be used to surveil Americans.

But wait, there's more to this bird-brained saga! As the dust settled on the great avian purge, a sinister twist emerged. The government, it seems, had a plan to fill the void left by our winged companions – enter the era of avian surveillance.

Yes, you heard that right. In place of our beloved birds, the skies were soon filled with avian-like robots, equipped with state-of-the-art surveillance technology. These feathered automatons flitted and fluttered, keeping a watchful eye on unsuspecting Americans below.

"It's like 'The Birds' meets '1984'," quipped one bewildered citizen upon hearing the news. "I always knew those pigeons were up to something suspicious!"

So, the next time you hear a chirp or catch a glimpse of a feathered friend overhead, spare a thought for the brave birds that once graced our skies. And who knows, that sparrow on your windowsill might just be keeping tabs on you for Uncle Sam!
